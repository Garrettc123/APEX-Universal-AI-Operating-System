## Phase 1: Legal Entity Formation (Days 1-3)

### Overview
Establish a legally recognized business entity with proper governance structure and compliance framework.

### Why This Matters
- Limited liability protection for personal assets
- Professional credibility with customers and partners
- Required for business banking and contracts
- Foundation for future funding rounds

### Action Items (42 steps)

#### 1.1 Choose Business Structure
**Timeline:** Day 1, Hour 1 (30 minutes)  
**Cost:** $0  
**Priority:** CRITICAL

**Steps:**
1. Review business structure options:
   - Sole Proprietorship (not recommended for tech)
   - LLC (good for small businesses)
   - S-Corp (good for small-medium)
   - C-Corp (required for VC funding) ‚úì RECOMMENDED
2. Understand tax implications of each
3. Consider future funding plans (C-Corp required for VC)
4. Evaluate liability protection needs
5. Consult with attorney if needed ($300-500)

**Decision:** Delaware C-Corporation  
**Reason:** Standard for tech startups, required for VC funding, best liability protection

#### 1.2 Select State of Incorporation
**Timeline:** Day 1, Hour 1 (15 minutes)  
**Cost:** $0  
**Priority:** CRITICAL

**Steps:**
1. Compare state options:
   - Delaware: Best for VC-backed startups ‚úì RECOMMENDED
   - Home state: Simpler but less flexible
   - Nevada: Tax benefits but complex
   - Wyoming: Low cost but limited advantages
2. Review Delaware advantages:
   - Court of Chancery (business-friendly)
   - No corporate income tax for out-of-state businesses
   - Strong legal precedents
   - VC/investor preference
3. Understand ongoing requirements ($300/year agent fee)

**Decision:** Delaware  
**Reason:** Industry standard for tech startups

## Phase 2: Tax Registration & Compliance (Days 2-4)

### Overview
Obtain federal and state tax identification numbers and establish compliance framework.

### Why This Matters

### Action Items (53 steps)

#### 2.1 Apply for Federal EIN
**Timeline:** Day 2, Hour 1 (15 minutes)  
**Cost:** $0 (free from IRS)  
**Priority:** CRITICAL

**What is an EIN?**
Employer Identification Number - federal tax ID for your business (like a SSN for companies)

**Application Methods:**
1. **Online** (recommended) ‚úì
   - https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online
   - Instant approval (receive EIN immediately)
   - Available Mon-Fri, 7am-10pm ET
   - Must have valid SSN or ITIN

2. **By Mail** (Form SS-4)
   - 4-6 weeks processing time
   - Not recommended due to delay

**Required Information:**
- Legal company name
- Trade name (DBA) if different
- Physical address (not P.O. Box)
- Responsible party name and SSN
- Business structure (C-Corporation)
- Reason for applying (started new business)
- Number of employees (0 initially)
- Principal business activity (Computer Software)

**Steps:**
1. Go to IRS EIN online application
2. Complete interview questions
3. Provide business information
4. Verify responsible party with SSN
5. Receive EIN instantly
6. Download and print confirmation (EIN Verification Letter)
7. Save securely - you'll need this constantly

**Action:** Apply online, receive EIN same day

#### 2.2 Order IRS Form CP575
**Timeline:** Day 2 (5 minutes)  
**Cost:** $0  
**Priority:** MEDIUM

**What is CP575?**
Official IRS confirmation letter for your EIN (some banks require this instead of online confirmation)

**How to Get:**
- Call IRS Business & Specialty Tax Line: (800) 829-4933
- Request CP575 for your EIN
- Receive by mail in 7-10 days

**Alternative:** Online confirmation usually sufficient, but order CP575 as backup

**Action:** Order by phone, use online confirmation in the meantime

#### 2.3 Register for State Taxes
**Timeline:** Day 3 (30 minutes per state)  
**Cost:** $0-50 per state  
**Priority:** HIGH

**Which States?**
1. **Delaware** (incorporation state) - no state income tax for out-of-state operations
2. **Your Home State** (if different from Delaware)
3. **States with Nexus** (employees, offices, or significant sales)

**Home State Registration:**
If operating from California example:
- Register with California Secretary of State ($70)
- File Statement of Information
- Obtain California corporation number

**When to Register:**
- Delaware: Not needed if no DE employees/offices
- Home state: Within 30 days of first business activity
- Other states: When nexus established

**Action:** Register in home state within 30 days

#### 2.4 Register for Sales Tax (if applicable)
**Timeline:** Day 3 (30 minutes)  
**Cost:** $0  
**Priority:** MEDIUM (varies by business model)

**Do You Need to Collect Sales Tax?**
- **SaaS products:** Increasingly taxable in many states
- **Professional services:** Generally not taxable
- **Physical products:** Always taxable

**Economic Nexus Thresholds (post-Wayfair):**
Most states: $100,000 in sales OR 200 transactions

**Steps:**
1. Determine if your product/service is taxable
2. Identify states where you have nexus
3. Register with each state's revenue department
4. Implement sales tax collection in billing system
5. File sales tax returns (monthly/quarterly)

**Tools:**
- TaxJar ($19-199/month) - automated sales tax
- Avalara ($50-500/month) - enterprise solution

**Action:** Research if SaaS is taxable in your states, register if needed

#### 2.5 Understand Franchise Tax Requirements
**Timeline:** Day 3 (15 minutes)  
**Cost:** Information gathering only  
**Priority:** MEDIUM

**What is Franchise Tax?**
Annual tax for the privilege of doing business in a state (separate from income tax)

**Delaware Franchise Tax:**
- **Due:** March 1 annually
- **Minimum:** $175 for up to 5,000 shares
- **Your Cost:** $400-500 (for 10M authorized shares)
- **Payment:** Online at Delaware Division of Corporations

**Calculation Methods:**
1. Authorized shares method (simpler)
2. Assumed par value capital method (usually cheaper)

**First Year:** Due March 1, 2027 (for 2026)

**Action:** Calendar reminder for February 1, 2027
